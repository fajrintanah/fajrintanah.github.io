Who We Are

About the Lab

The Computational Soil Science (CSS) is an Independent research group based in Dramaga, Bogor. CSS is dedicated to advancing our understanding of soil systems through cutting-edge computational methods, machine learning, and spatial data analysis.

We integrate field measurements, laboratory data, and remote sensing to build predictive models that help address global challenges in food security, carbon cycling, environment and land management.

Get in Touch

Machine Learning

Applying ML models to predict soil properties and greenhouse gas emissions from large-scale datasets.

Remote Sensing

Using satellite imagery to map soil variability across landscapes.

Data Science

Building robust pipelines for soil data integration and analysis.

Spatial Analysis

GIS-based approaches for understanding spatial patterns of soil health.

What We Do

Research

Featured

Data-driven Groundwater Level Prediction
and CO₂ Emission Estimation in Tropical Peatlands

Integrates field observations, remote sensing, environmental covariates, and machine learning to predict spatiotemporal groundwater dynamics and estimate associated CO₂ emissions across tropical peatland landscapes bounded by unified hydrological systems.

Ongoing

Soil Macro- and Micronutrient Modeling in Cultivated Tropical Peat Using Artificial Intelligence

Applies artificial intelligence and environmental data to model, map, and understand the spatial variability of macro- and micronutrient availability in cultivated tropical peat soils for sustainable land management.

Active

Developing Intelligent System for Computing Soil Properties and Greenhouse Gas Fluxes from Tropical Peatlands

Develops an intelligent decision-support framework that combines soil science, sensor data, remote sensing, and machine learning to estimate soil properties and greenhouse gas fluxes in tropical peatland ecosystems.
