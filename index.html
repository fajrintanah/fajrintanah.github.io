Who We Are

About the Lab

The Computational Soil Science (CSS) Lab is dedicated to advancing our understanding of soil systems through cutting-edge computational methods, machine learning, and spatial data analysis.

We integrate field measurements, laboratory data, and remote sensing to build predictive models that help address global challenges in food security, carbon cycling, and land management.

Get in Touch

Machine Learning

Applying ML models to predict soil properties from large-scale datasets.

Remote Sensing

Using satellite and drone imagery to map soil variability across landscapes.

Data Science

Building robust pipelines for soil data integration and analysis.

Spatial Analysis

GIS-based approaches for understanding spatial patterns of soil health.

What We Do

Research Focus

Soil Carbon Modeling

Developing computational models to quantify and predict soil organic carbon stocks under different land use and climate scenarios.

Ongoing

Digital Soil Mapping

Creating high-resolution spatial maps of soil properties using machine learning and remote sensing data.

Active

Soil Spectroscopy

Using Vis-NIR and MIR spectroscopy combined with chemometrics for rapid soil analysis.

Active

Soil–Water Dynamics

Simulating water flow and solute transport through soil profiles under varying climate conditions.

Active

Predictive Pedology

Combining traditional soil science with data-driven approaches for soil classification and genesis.
